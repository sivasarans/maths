Time, Work & Efficiency

Bank Exam Prep
Time & Work Efficiency Ratio Method LCM Method
Core Formulas
Work (W)W = E × T
Efficiency (E)E = W ÷ T
Time (T)T = W ÷ E
Efficiency of A (x days)1 / x
A+B Time1 / (1/x + 1/y)
Efficiency ↑ k%Time ↓ k/(100+k)
WHY it works:
Efficiency means work per day. If a person finishes work in 10 days, he completes 1/10 work daily. More efficiency → less time. Time and efficiency are always inversely proportional.
Simple Remember Tips:
Efficiency means Speed.
Work means Distance
Q1 • Basic FormulaConcept
If efficiency is 1/10 work per day, how much work is done in 4 days?
Work = Efficiency × Time = (1/10) × 4 = 2/5
Q2 • Find EfficiencyBasic
A completes a work in 8 days. What is his efficiency?
Efficiency = 1 / Time = 1/8
Q3 • Find TimeConcept
Efficiency of A is 1/5. How many days will A take to complete the work?
Time = 1 ÷ (1/5) = 5 days
Q4 • Combined WorkApplication
A can do work in 10 days and B in 15 days. How long will they take together?
1/10 + 1/15 = 1/6 → Time = 6 days
Q5 • A+B+CAdvanced
A, B, C can complete a work in 6, 12, 4 days respectively. Together they take?
1/6+1/12+1/4 = 1/2 → Time = 2 days
Q6 • Find IndividualTricky
A and B together take 6 days. A alone takes 10 days. B alone takes?
B = 1/6 − 1/10 = 1/15 → 15 days
Q7 • Ratio MethodFast
Efficiency ratio of A:B is 2:3. Time ratio is?
Time ratio is inverse of efficiency
Q8 • LCM MethodSafe
A and B take 12 and 18 days. Using LCM, total work is?
LCM(12,18) = 36 units
Q9 • Partial WorkApplication
A does work in 10 days, B in 20 days. A works 4 days, B 2 days. Work done?
2/5 + 1/10 = 1/2
Q10 • Efficiency IncreasePercent
Efficiency increases by 25%. Time will decrease by?
25/(100+25) ×100 = 20%
Q11 • Men Days HoursStandard
10 men work 8 days, 6 hrs/day. How many days for 20 men at 6 hrs/day?
10×8×6 = 20×D×6 → D=4
Q12 • Alternate DaysTricky
A works alternate days with B. A=10 days, B=20 days. Remaining work after 6 days?
Work done = 9/20 → Remaining = 11/20
Q13 • WagesExam Gold
A works 10 days, B 5 days. Efficiency A:B = 2:1. Wage ratio?
(10×2):(5×1) = 20:5 = 4:1
