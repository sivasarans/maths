WHY Alligation Works:
The mean price lies between cheaper and dearer values. Difference decides the quantity ratio directly.
REMEMBER:
✔ Always subtract diagonally ✔ Ratio is inverse of difference ✔ Replacement → exponential decay
RATIO & PROPORTION (Foundation):
A ratio shows comparison between two quantities.
If quantities are x and y → Ratio = x : y
• x = Antecedent
• y = Consequent

Proportion means two ratios are equal:
a : b :: c : d ⇒ a/b = c/d
Example: 6 : 8 :: 3 : 4
TYPES OF MIXTURES:
1. Simple Mixture – mixing pure items directly
Example: Milk + Water, Rice + Rice

2. Complex Mixture – mixing mixtures with mixtures
Example: Alloy A + Alloy B
WHAT IS ALLIGATION?
Alligation is a shortcut method to find the ratio in which items must be mixed to get a desired mean value.

It is based on the concept of Weighted Average.
WEIGHTED AVERAGE CONCEPT:
If quantities n₁, n₂ with values A₁, A₂ are mixed,
Average = (n₁A₁ + n₂A₂) / (n₁ + n₂)
ALLIGATION RULE (MOST IMPORTANT):
Quantity of Cheaper : Quantity of Dearer =
(Dearer − Mean) : (Mean − Cheaper)

✔ Mean always lies between cheaper & dearer ✔ Bigger quantity is taken of the value closer to mean
ALLIGATION TYPES:
1. Alligation Medial
Used to find mean value when quantities are given.

2. Alligation Alternate
Used to find ratio of quantities when mean is given.
GRAPHICAL (CROSS) METHOD:
Cheaper —— Mean —— Dearer
Cross subtract diagonally to get ratio.
Fastest method in exams.
IMPORTANT PROPERTIES:
✔ Mean value always lies between two values
✔ Quantity ∝ distance from mean
✔ Value × Quantity is additive
✔ Same logic works for cost, %, purity, strength
REPLACEMENT CONCEPT:
If x is replaced from volume V, n times:
Pure left = V(1 − x/V)ⁿ

Used in Milk–Water, Petrol–Kerosene problems.
IMPORTANT RATIO RULES (EXAM GOLD):
✔ Componendo: (a+b)/b = (c+d)/d
✔ Dividendo: (a−b)/b = (c−d)/d
✔ Invertendo: b/a = d/c
✔ Alternendo: a/c = b/d
EXAM STRATEGY:
• If mean given → Alligation
• If replaced repeatedly → Formula
• If two mixtures mixed → Equalize quantity first
• If selling involved → Find CP of mixture first
Q1 • RatioFoundation
Milk and water are mixed in the ratio 3:2. What fraction of the mixture is milk?
Milk part = 3 / (3+2) = 3/5
Q2 • AverageMedial
4 kg sugar at ₹30/kg and 6 kg sugar at ₹50/kg are mixed. Find mean price.
(4×30 + 6×50) / 10 = ₹42
Q3 • AlligationAlternate
Rice at ₹20/kg is mixed with rice at ₹40/kg to get mixture at ₹30/kg. Ratio?
(40−30):(30−20)=10:10=1:1
Q4 • PercentageConcept
10L of 20% alcohol mixed with 10L of 40% alcohol. Final strength?
(10×20 + 10×40)/20 = 30%
Q5 • ReplacementOnce
A vessel has 20L milk. 5L is replaced with water. Milk left?
Milk left = 20×(1−5/20)=15L
Q6 • ReplacementRepeated
20L milk, 5L replaced twice. Milk left?
20×(3/4)² = 11.25L
Q7 • ComplexMixtures
Mixtures A and B contain milk & water in ratios 2:1 and 3:2. Equal quantities mixed. Final ratio?
Milk = 2/3+3/5 = 13/15, Water = 7/15
Q8 • ProfitSelling
Sugar at ₹30 & ₹50 mixed in ratio 1:1, sold at ₹48. Profit %?
CP=40, Profit=8 → 8/40×100=20%
Q9 • PurityConcept
A mixture has 80% milk. How much water is there in 25L?
Water = 20% of 25 = 5L
Q10 • FractionRatio
If water is 3/8 of a mixture, milk fraction is?
Milk = 1 − 3/8 = 5/8
Q11 • ConceptMean
Mean price of mixture will always lie?
Mean always lies between two values
Q12 • TrickAlligation
Mean closer to cheaper means quantity of cheaper is?
Closer value contributes more quantity
Q13 • AdvancedReplacement
After 3 replacements of 1/5 part, fraction of original left?
(1−1/5)³ = (4/5)³
Q14 • Ratio RuleBasics
If a:b = 2:3, then a/(a+b) equals?
a/(a+b) = 2/(2+3)=2/5
